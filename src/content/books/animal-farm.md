One of my "tent books." This was the most lightweight book I had with me on a recent backpacking trip, and was therefore the one that got stashed in my pack. Good call, Luke.

I read Animal Farm in my tent during a rain storm in Logan Canyon, Utah. Some of my immediate takeaways:

- Wow. This feels incredibly relevant today. I guess maybe its one of those books that will always feel relevant. Orwell was incredibly prescient at identifying this pattern of "if something is said enough times, it will become true" that has come to dominate western politics and culture.
- Poor Boxer, man. 
- I have been wondering who I identify with in this book. In 1984, Orwell obviously intends for the reader to put themselves into Winston's shoes. But this book has no comparable protagonist. Maybe Benjamin or Boxer.
- My hot take: this would be more valuable for students to read than 1984. Orwell saw the writing on the wall, maybe better than anybody, about where totalitarianism would eventually go. Some of the Stalin and Trotsky references went over my head but I think this is a book everyone can take something from.
